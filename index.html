Onboarding Checklists
👤 New Consumer Onboarding Checklist

All documents are collected via GoHighLevel e-sign. No in-person meeting required — annual home visit is the only required in-person contact.

STEP 1 — ELIGIBILITY & AUTHORIZATION
1
Receive & Date-Stamp Authorization — Clock Starts Now
Stamp the date received on the authorization immediately upon receipt. Services must begin within 7 calendar days (or the authorization start date, whichever is later). If you cannot start in time, place written justification in the consumer's file and send a copy to the DHSS case manager. Confirm consumer is MO HealthNet eligible, 18+, able to self-direct care.
Reg: 19 CSR 15-8.400 §5; P&P Policy 5
2
Review DHSS-Approved Plan of Care
Obtain the approved plan of care from DHSS/DSDS. Note the authorized hours, units, and specific tasks. Services must be delivered strictly per the care plan — do not provide any task not authorized.
Reg: CDS Program Req. §2.2
3
Verify TPL Insurance & Medicare Before Proceeding
Ask the consumer about other insurance coverage before billing MO HealthNet. If they have private insurance or Medicare, that must be billed first — MO HealthNet is payer of last resort. Verify via IVR: (573) 635-8908 or emomed.com. Document TPL status in consumer file.
Reg: P&P Policy 9; CDS Program Req. §3.3(c)
STEP 2 — CONSUMER ENROLLMENT PACKET (GoHighLevel)
4
Send Consumer to caringcds.com/onboarding — Two-Step GoHighLevel Flow
Direct the consumer to caringcds.com/onboarding. This is a connected two-step GoHighLevel flow — their basic info prefills all documents automatically:
  • Step 1 (caringcds.com/onboarding) — Consumer fills out basic info survey; info prefills all enrollment documents
  • Step 2 (caringcds.com/onboarding-packet) — Consumer reviews and e-signs all prefilled enrollment documents, including: Consumer Vendor Acknowledgement, Authorization to Obtain Employment Numbers, Previous CDS Vendor Authorization (PCCP transfers), Telephony/EVV Consent Form, Consumer Emergency Back-Up Plan, Form 8821, Form SS-4, Form 2678, Form 2643A, Form 2827
⚠️ Do NOT send the Training Acknowledgement Form in this packet — consumer must complete orientation first (Step 3 below).
Reg: CDS Program Req. §2.3(e); P&P Policy 3
STEP 3 — CONSUMER ORIENTATION (Before Training Ack)
5
Direct Consumer to Complete Online Orientation
Send consumer to caringcds.com/orientation to:
  • Watch the CDS orientation videos
  • Review the CDS Handbook
The orientation covers all required training topics: consumer eligibility, plan of care, consumer responsibilities and rights, what consumers are not allowed to do, emergency backup plan, maintaining eligibility, denial/termination/reduction, grievance procedures, disaster policy, how to recruit/hire/train/supervise attendants, vendor responsibilities, required background checks, attendant qualifications and responsibilities, mandated reporting, fraud prevention, HIPAA, EVV, allowable/non-allowable tasks, and essential transportation.
Reg: 19 CSR 15-8.400; MMAC Vendor Responsibilities; P&P Policy 7
6
Send Training Acknowledgement Form via GoHighLevel (After Orientation)
After consumer has watched the videos and reviewed the handbook, send the Consumer Training Acknowledgement Form via GoHighLevel for e-signature. This form documents that the consumer has been trained on all required CDS program topics. Acknowledgement form must be placed in consumer file. If consumer needs help, offer to walk them through orientation by phone.
Reg: CDS Program Req. §5.18; P&P Policy 7
STEP 4 — PROCESS TAX FORMS (After Signed Packet Received)
7
Submit SS-4 — Obtain Federal EIN for Consumer
Using the signed SS-4 and Authorization to Obtain Employment Numbers, obtain the consumer's Federal EIN online via irs.gov. Consumer is registered as a "Household Employer (HCSR)." Record EIN in consumer file.
8
Submit Form 8821 & Form 2678 to IRS
File Form 8821 (Tax Information Authorization) and Form 2678 (Appointment of Agent) with the IRS. These authorize Caring Companions to handle federal payroll tax reporting and payments on the consumer's behalf. Form 2678 covers Forms 940 and 941.
9
Submit Form 2643A & Form 2827 to Missouri
Submit MO Form 2643A (Tax Registration — "New Consumer-Directed Services Participant") to MO Department of Revenue. Submit Form 2827 (MO Power of Attorney — Withholding) to authorize Caring Companions for state tax matters. Mail to: Taxation Division, P.O. Box 357, Jefferson City MO 65105-0357. Note consumer's MO Tax ID when received.
MO Revenue: (573) 751-5860 | businesstaxregister@dor.mo.gov
10
Register Consumer in UInteract (DES) — Missouri UI Tax Only
⚠️ Timing: UInteract registration can ONLY happen AFTER first payroll is issued AND consumer has paid at least $1,000 in wages in a single quarter. Do not attempt before then — DES will not issue an account number.

NEW Consumer (never had a CDS vendor before):
Login to uinteract.labor.mo.gov → Employer Registration → Register Employer. Enter consumer's EIN and SSN. Type of Organization = "Individual Ownership." Business type = "Health Care / Social Assistance / Services for Elderly and Persons with Disabilities." Domestic employment = Yes. Enter consumer's home address as headquarters. Enter wage history (gross wages per quarter). Submit. You will receive the consumer's MO Unemployment Tax Account Number (EAN).

TRANSFERRED Consumer (came from another CDS vendor):
Do NOT use UInteract to register. Instead, email MODES-5083 (Transferred Consumer Registration form) to cdstax@labor.mo.gov after their first payroll with you. Need: consumer's EIN, SSN, first payroll date with you, name of previous vendor. Download form: labor.mo.gov/media/22536/download

After registration: File quarterly wage reports per consumer (Q1 Apr 30 / Q2 Jul 30 / Q3 Oct 31 / Q4 Jan 31). Wages reported under consumer's account — NEVER under Caring Companions' account. Separate payment per consumer — do NOT combine.

Parent attendant exception: If attendant is a parent of the consumer, wages are NEVER reportable for UI purposes — do not report them.
General questions: cdstax@labor.mo.gov | 573-751-1995 Opt. 1 | Delinquent/penalties: Christopher Matzke 573-751-1867 | Wage reporting/adjustments: Kristie Riedmann 573-751-1995 x4105
10b
Local Tax Registration — Kansas City or St. Louis Consumers Only
If the consumer lives within Kansas City or St. Louis city limits, register for local payroll taxes. Not required for other Missouri locations.
STEP 5 — SETUP & GO-LIVE
11
Set Up Consumer in WellSky Personal Care (EVV & Billing)
Enroll consumer in WellSky Personal Care — our EVV AND billing platform. Confirm attendant and consumer both understand EVV clock-in/clock-out procedure and the Saturday 11:59 PM weekly correction deadline. All MO HealthNet billing flows through WellSky. Portal: mycaringcds.com
12
Establish Consumer Case File
Create complete consumer file with all of the following:
  • Date-stamped authorization + Plan of Care
  • All signed GoHighLevel e-sign documents (Vendor Ack, Auth to Obtain Numbers, Previous Vendor Auth, EVV Consent, Training Ack)
  • Signed Emergency Back-Up Plan (names + phone numbers of backup caregiver)
  • TPL/insurance documentation
  • All tax form copies (SS-4, 8821, 2678, 2643A, 2827)
  • EVV and WellSky enrollment confirmation
Reg: CDS Program Req. §5.23; P&P Policy 6
13
Schedule First Monthly Case Management Contact
Begin monthly monitoring cadence — phone or in-person. Document all contacts in consumer file. The required annual face-to-face home visit counts as a contact; schedule it within the first year.
Reg: 19 CSR 15-8.400(4)(F)
🧑‍🤝‍🧑 New Attendant Onboarding Checklist

All documents collected via Empeon e-sign. No in-person meeting required. Complete ALL steps before attendant begins providing services — no exceptions.

STEP 1 — INTAKE (Attendant Self-Serve)
1
Invite Attendant to Empeon — Complete All Hiring Paperwork Digitally
Invite attendant into Empeon. Attendant completes all hiring paperwork digitally via e-sign: application, W-4, I-9, direct deposit, attendant contract, and all required disclosures. Application must capture: (a) disclosure of ALL criminal convictions/pleas; (b) consent to criminal record check; (c) consent to closed records check (§610.120 RSMo); (d) disclosure of ALL aliases and SSNs used. Do not run background checks or approve attendant until signed paperwork is returned.
Platform: Empeon | Reg: CDS Program Req. §4.2.1
STEP 2 — PRE-HIRE SCREENING (After Signed Packet Received)
3
FCSR — Family Care Safety Registry Check
Screen ALL aliases and ALL SSNs disclosed. Check for: criminal history, sex offender registry, child abuse records, DHSS EDL, DMH EDR, child care license revocations. Retain documentation on file. If finding — verify whether disqualifying under RSMo 192.2495.
Reg: CDS Program Req. §4.1; 19 CSR 30-82.060
4
EDL — Employee Disqualification List Check
Check Missouri EDL at health.mo.gov/safety/edl — screen ALL known aliases. Do NOT hire anyone listed. Retain documentation.
Reg: RSMo 192.2490; CDS Program Req. §4.1
4
LEIE — OIG Exclusion List Check
Screen at exclusions.oig.hhs.gov. Screen ALL known aliases. Retain proof. Any exclusion discovered must be reported to MMAC immediately.
Reg: CDS Program Req. §5.2
5
Out-of-State Check — Has Attendant Lived Outside Missouri in Last 5 Years?
If YES: FCSR data is Missouri-only — you must also obtain background checks (criminal history, sex offender, child abuse/neglect registry) from each state the attendant lived in during the past 5 years. Additionally, a fingerprint-based background check is required for a national FBI criminal history search via Missouri State Highway Patrol (MSHP). Submit FBI FD-258 fingerprint card to MSHP CJIS Division, PO Box 9500, Jefferson City, MO 65102-9500 (4–6 week processing), or use IDEMIA (MO's fingerprint vendor) at (844) 543-9712. Out-of-state applicants can be fingerprinted by local law enforcement in their state. If NO (MO resident 5+ years): Standard FCSR check is sufficient for state criminal history.
MSHP CJIS Division: 573-526-6153 | IDEMIA: 844-543-9712 | FCSR: Missouri data only
6
Verify Attendant is NOT Consumer's Spouse
Attendant can be a family member (parent, sibling, child, grandparent) but CANNOT be the consumer's spouse or legal guardian. Obtain verification if needed.
Reg: §208.909; CDS Program Req. §4.4.1
6
E-Verify — Work Authorization
If agency qualifies as a "business entity" under RSMo 285.525, must enroll and participate in E-Verify. Run E-Verify on all new hires.
Reg: CDS Program Req. §4.5; RSMo 285.530
STEP 3 — HIRE DOCUMENTATION (After All Checks Clear)
8
Confirm All Checks Cleared — Approve in Empeon
Only after FCSR + EDL + OIG/LEIE all return clear (and fingerprint check if out-of-state) — mark attendant as approved in Empeon and allow them to begin serving a consumer.

⚠️ FCSR Disqualifying Finding? If FCSR returns a disqualifying offense, the attendant CANNOT work under ANY circumstances until a Good Cause Waiver (GCW) is applied for AND approved by DHSS. Conditional employment is never permitted while awaiting GCW. If GCW is denied — the attendant cannot be hired. Reg: P&P Policy 13; CDS Program Req. §4.3
9
Execute Attendant Contract
Agency must have a contract with each personal care attendant. Even though the attendant is the consumer's employee, the vendor maintains the contract.
Reg: CDS Program Req. §4.4.1
10
Report New Hire to State of Missouri
Report new hire to Missouri Division of Employment Security (new hire reporting requirement). File under consumer's EIN.
11
Obtain FCSR Registration Number
Obtain attendant's FCSR number — required for EVV system. Record in attendant file.
11b
⚠️ Issue & Mail Permanent Agency ID Card to Attendant
This is a gap currently being implemented. At time of employment, mail a permanent ID card to the attendant. Card must display: (1) Agency name (Caring Companions), (2) Attendant's full name, (3) Title (Personal Care Attendant), (4) Attendant's signature. Attendant must carry this card at all times while working. Upon termination: request card be returned by mail. If unable to recover, document recovery attempts in writing and place in file.
Reg: P&P Policy 6 & 8; CDS Program Req. §5.6
STEP 4 — TRAINING & SETUP
12
Train Attendant on WellSky Personal Care (EVV)
Attendant must understand how to use WellSky Personal Care for check-in/check-out (portal: mycaringcds.com). EVV Correction Forms due by Saturday 11:59 PM each week. Train on fraud consequences of falsifying EVV. Attendant's FCSR# must be captured in EVV.
Reg: 13 CSR 70-3.320
13
Orient Attendant on Consumer's Care Plan
Attendant should understand the consumer's authorized tasks, hours, and limitations. Services must be delivered per the approved care plan only.
14
Orient Attendant on Fraud Prevention
Do not leave early without adjusting EVV. Do not claim hours not worked. Do not use EVV when consumer is hospitalized. False claims = fraud.
15
Payroll Setup Under Consumer's EIN (via Empeon)
Set up attendant in payroll system under the consumer's EIN (NOT Caring Companions' EIN 82-0968875). Pay rate: $15/hour. Direct deposit processes Thursdays (Wed before major holidays). Withhold and remit FICA, Medicare, federal and state income tax. Register attendant wages under consumer's UInteract account at uinteract.labor.mo.gov.
16
Establish LEIE Monthly Screening Schedule
Add attendant to monthly LEIE screening roster. Screen all aliases monthly. Document each screening.
Reg: CDS Program Req. §5.2 — Monthly requirement, non-negotiable
STEP 5 — ONGOING (POST-HIRE)
17
EDL Quarterly Re-Check
Re-screen all attendants against EDL every quarter. Document date and results.
Reg: RSMo 192.2490
18
FCSR Annual Re-Screen (Recommended)
DHSS recommends annual FCSR re-screening of all employed attendants. Document per PM-VM 17-31 guidance.

📌 EVV Correction Policy & Acceptable Reasons
✅ Acceptable Reasons for Manual Correction
  • Phone was dead / no battery at clock-in or out
  • App crashed or technical error (screenshot recommended)
  • No cell service at consumer's location (rural area)
  • Attendant forgot to clock in or out (occasional — not recurring)
  • WellSky app update caused disruption
  • Consumer's home phone not available for landline EVV
🚫 Red Flags — Patterns That Trigger MMAC Scrutiny
  • Same attendant requesting corrections repeatedly
  • Corrections that extend shift time (not just fix a missed clock)
  • Corrections submitted days after the visit (not same day)
  • No signed correction form on file for an adjustment
  • 3+ corrections in one month for the same attendant
  • 10+ corrections agency-wide in one month
  • Pattern of corrections always on the same day/consumer
⚠️ Documentation Rule: Every manual adjustment in WellSky must have a corresponding signed EVV Correction Form on file before the correction is made. If MMAC audits a claim and there is no form, the adjustment looks fraudulent. Saturday 11:59 PM weekly deadline still applies — corrections for the week must be in before that cutoff.

⚠️ Tax & Enrollment Setup

Tax & Enrollment Setup
STEP 1 — Get Consumer's Federal EIN (IRS Form SS-4)
The consumer is their own employer and needs their own EIN — separate from Caring Companions' EIN.
1. Go to irs.gov → Tools → Apply for an EIN Online (free, instant).
2. Entity type: Individual / Sole Proprietor.
3. Reason: Hired employees.
4. Use the consumer's SSN, legal name, and home address.
5. Complete online — you'll receive the EIN immediately. Print and save the confirmation.
6. Enter the EIN in the field below.
STEP 2 — IRS Form 8821 (Tax Information Authorization)
This authorizes Caring Companions to receive the consumer's IRS tax correspondence and speak to the IRS on their behalf.
⚠️ Wet/faxed signature required — do NOT rely on e-sign until you confirm with your accountant.
1. Consumer signs Form 8821 with their name and EIN.
2. Mail or fax to IRS per the form's instructions.
3. Keep a copy in the consumer's file.
STEP 3 — IRS Form 2678 (Employer/Payer Agent Appointment)
This designates Caring Companions as the agent authorized to handle FICA and FUTA payroll taxes on the consumer's behalf. IRS must approve — do not process payroll before approval.
⚠️ Wet/faxed signature required. IRS approval can take several weeks.
1. Complete Form 2678 with consumer's EIN and Caring Companions' EIN.
2. Consumer and Caring Companions both sign.
3. Mail to IRS. Wait for approval letter before first payroll run.
STEP 4A — MO Form 2643A (Missouri Tax Registration)
Registers the consumer with the Missouri Department of Revenue for state income tax withholding.
1. Complete Form 2643A using consumer's EIN, SSN, name, and home address.
2. Business type: Individual / Household employer.
3. Submit online at dor.mo.gov (preferred) or mail to MO Dept of Revenue.
4. You will receive the consumer's MO Withholding Tax ID — enter it in the MO Tax ID field below.
Phone: (573) 751-5860 | businesstaxregister@dor.mo.gov
STEP 4B — MO Form 2827 (Missouri Power of Attorney)
Authorizes Caring Companions to act on behalf of the consumer with the MO Department of Revenue for withholding tax matters.
1. Consumer signs Form 2827 with their MO Tax ID and EIN.
2. Submit to MO Dept of Revenue.
3. Keep a copy in the consumer's file.
STEP 5 — DES / UInteract (Missouri Unemployment Insurance Tax)
⚠️ TIMING: Cannot do this step until AFTER first payroll is issued AND consumer has paid $1,000+ in wages in a single quarter. DES will not issue an account number until then.

If NEW consumer (never had a CDS vendor before):
1. Login to uinteract.labor.mo.gov with your CDS Vendor credentials.
2. Click Employer Registration → Register Employer. Enter YOUR info as the submitter.
3. Enter CONSUMER's EIN. Type of Organization = Individual Ownership.
4. Business activity: Health Care → Social Assistance → Services for Elderly and Persons with Disabilities.
5. Domestic employment = Yes. Enter consumer's home address as headquarters address.
6. Telephone: 000-000-0000 (consumer is never contacted). Enter YOUR info as the contact.
7. Enter gross wage history per quarter. Enter attendant count per week.
8. Submit. You receive the consumer's EAN (Employer Account Number) — record it below.

If TRANSFERRED consumer (came from another CDS vendor):
1. Download MODES-5083 from labor.mo.gov/media/22536/download
2. Fill in: your name/EIN, consumer's EIN + SSN, first payroll date with you, name of prior vendor.
3. Email (do NOT mail or fax) to cdstax@labor.mo.gov.

After registration — quarterly wage reporting:
Login → Contribution and Wage Report → File Contribution and Wage Report. File oldest quarter first. Select Method A. Enter attendant SSN, name, and gross wages per quarter. Separate payment per consumer — never combine. If parent is the attendant: do NOT report those wages (not subject to UI tax).

Quarterly deadlines: Q1 (Jan–Mar): Apr 30 | Q2 (Apr–Jun): Jul 30 | Q3 (Jul–Sep): Oct 31 | Q4 (Oct–Dec): Jan 31
Late penalty: 10% of tax or $100/month, whichever is greater — max 20% or $200.
